📐 Formula Cheat Sheet (Quick Reference)
Motion: v = d/t | a = Δv/t | v_f = v_i + at
Forces: F = ma | W = mg | F_f = μF_n
Gravity/Circular: F_g = Gm₁m₂/r² | a_c = v²/r | F_c = mv²/r
Energy: W = Fd | KE = ½mv² | PE = mgh | P = W/t
Momentum: p = mv | J = FΔt = Δp
Electricity: F_e = kq₁q₂/r² | V = IR | P = IV
Waves: v = fλ | T = 1/f

1. Momentum Calc: A 0.5 kg ball is moving at 10 m/s. What is its momentum?

Answer
p = mv = 0.5 * 10 = 5 kg·m/s.
Easy

2. Impulse: What happens to the momentum of an object when an impulse is applied to it?

Answer
The momentum changes. Impulse = Change in Momentum.

Medium

3. Collisions: Two billiard balls collide and bounce off each other perfectly. What kind of collision is this?

Answer
Elastic Collision (both momentum and kinetic energy are conserved).
Medium

4. Impact Time: Why do cars have crumple zones? (Explain using Impulse = Force * Time).

Answer
Crumple zones increase the time of the impact. Since the change in momentum is fixed, increasing the time decreases the impact force on the passengers.

Hard

5. Recoil: A 5 kg cannon fires a 0.1 kg ball at 100 m/s. What is the recoil velocity of the cannon?

Answer
Total momentum before = 0. After: 0 = (m_cannon * v_cannon) + (m_ball * v_ball).
0 = (5 * v_c) + (0.1 * 100) → 5v_c = -10 → v_c = -2 m/s (backwards).
