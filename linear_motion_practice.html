1. Newton's 2nd Law: A 10 kg box is pushed with a net force of 50 N. What is the acceleration of the box?

Answer
F = ma → a = F/m = 50N / 10kg = 5 m/s²
Easy

2. Weight: What is the weight (force of gravity) of a 5 kg bowling ball on Earth? (g = 9.8 m/s²)

Answer
W = mg = 5kg * 9.8 m/s² = 49 N
Easy

3. Inertia: According to Newton's 1st Law, if no net force acts on a moving object, what happens to its velocity?

Answer
The object will continue moving with constant velocity (same speed and direction) indefinitely.

🟡 Medium

Medium

4. Friction: A 20 kg crate is pushed with 100 N of force, but there is 40 N of friction opposing it. What is the acceleration?

Answer
F_net = 100N - 40N = 60N.
a = F_net / m = 60N / 20kg = 3 m/s²
Medium

5. Projectile Launch: A ball is thrown horizontally off a cliff at 10 m/s. How far does it move horizontally after 3 seconds?

Answer
Horizontal velocity is constant. d = v * t = 10 m/s * 3s = 30 meters.
Medium

6. Free-Body Diagram: An object is falling at its terminal velocity. What is the relationship between the force of gravity and the force of air resistance?

Answer
At terminal velocity, acceleration is 0. This means the force of gravity equals the force of air resistance (they cancel out).

Hard

7. Action-Reaction: A bug hits the windshield of a fast-moving bus. Which object experiences a greater force: the bug or the bus? Explain using Newton's 3rd Law.

Answer
According to Newton's 3rd Law, the forces are exactly equal in magnitude. The bug squashes more because it has much less mass, so it experiences a much higher acceleration.
Hard

8. Vertical Projectile: A rock is dropped from a bridge and hits the water 4 seconds later. How high is the bridge? (Ignore air resistance, g = 9.8 m/s²)

Answer
Use d = 1/2 * g * t²
d = 0.5 * 9.8 * 4² = 0.5 * 9.8 * 16 = 78.4 meters.
Hard

9. Inclined Plane: A 10 kg block is sitting on a frictionless ramp inclined at 30 degrees. What is the force component pulling it down the ramp? (Hint: F = mg * sinθ)

Answer
F_parallel = mg * sin(30°)
F = 10kg * 9.8 m/s² * 0.5 = 49 N.
