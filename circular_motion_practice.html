1. Direction of Force: In uniform circular motion, what is the direction of the net force acting on the object?

Answer
The force (Centripetal Force) is always directed toward the center of the circle.
Easy

2. Gravitational Attraction: According to Newton, what two factors determine the strength of gravity between two objects?

Answer
The masses of the objects and the distance between them.

🟡 Medium

Medium

3. Centripetal Force: A 2 kg ball is swung in a circle with a radius of 1 meter at a speed of 4 m/s. What is the centripetal force?

Answer
F = mv² / r = (2 * 4²) / 1 = 2 * 16 = 32 N.
Medium

4. Inverse Square Law: If you double the distance between two planets, what happens to the gravitational force between them?

Answer
Force follows the inverse square law (1/r²). If r doubles, force becomes (1/2)² = 1/4th (one-quarter) of the original force.

🔴 Hard

Hard

5. Satellite Motion: Why doesn't the Moon fall into the Earth if Earth's gravity is pulling on it?

Answer
The Moon has high tangential velocity. Gravity pulls it into a curve, but it is moving fast enough "sideways" that it constantly falls around the Earth rather than into it.
