🔋 Mechanical Energy & Work

📐 Formula Cheat Sheet (Quick Reference)
Motion: v = d/t | a = Δv/t | v_f = v_i + at
Forces: F = ma | W = mg | F_f = μF_n
Gravity/Circular: F_g = Gm₁m₂/r² | a_c = v²/r | F_c = mv²/r
Energy: W = Fd | KE = ½mv² | PE = mgh | P = W/t
Momentum: p = mv | J = FΔt = Δp
Electricity: F_e = kq₁q₂/r² | V = IR | P = IV
Waves: v = fλ | T = 1/f

🟢 Easy

Easy

1. Work Definition: You push against a wall with 500 N of force for 1 hour, but the wall doesn't move. How much work was done?

Answer
Work = Force * Displacement. Since d = 0, Work = 0 Joules.
Easy

2. PE Formula: A 2 kg book is held 3 meters above the floor. Calculate its potential energy (g = 9.8).

Answer
PE = mgh = 2 * 9.8 * 3 = 58.8 Joules.

🟡 Medium

Medium

3. Kinetic Energy: A 1000 kg car moves at 10 m/s. What is its kinetic energy?

Answer
KE = 1/2 mv² = 0.5 * 1000 * 10² = 500 * 100 = 50,000 Joules.
Medium

4. Conservation: A 1 kg ball is at the top of a 5-meter tall frictionless ramp. What will be its kinetic energy at the bottom?

Answer
PE_top = mgh = 1 * 9.8 * 5 = 49J. By conservation of energy, all PE turns to KE. KE = 49 Joules.

🔴 Hard

Hard

5. Power: An engine lifts a 200 kg crate 10 meters in 5 seconds. Calculate the power output.

Answer
1. Work = Force * distance = (200 * 9.8) * 10 = 19,600J.
2. Power = Work / Time = 19,600 / 5 = 3,920 Watts.
