📐 Formula Cheat Sheet (Quick Reference)
Motion: v = d/t | a = Δv/t | v_f = v_i + at
Forces: F = ma | W = mg | F_f = μF_n
Gravity/Circular: F_g = Gm₁m₂/r² | a_c = v²/r | F_c = mv²/r
Energy: W = Fd | KE = ½mv² | PE = mgh | P = W/t
Momentum: p = mv | J = FΔt = Δp
Electricity: F_e = kq₁q₂/r² | V = IR | P = IV
Waves: v = fλ | T = 1/f

1. Wave Speed Formula: A wave has a frequency of 2 Hz and a wavelength of 5 meters. What is its speed?

Answer
v = f * λ = 2 * 5 = 10 m/s.
Easy

2. Medium: Can sound waves travel through a vacuum (empty space)? Why or why not?

Answer
No. Sound is a mechanical wave and requires a medium (like air or water) to vibrate and travel.

🟡 Medium

Medium

3. Transverse vs. Longitudinal: In which type of wave do the particles move parallel to the direction of wave travel?

Answer
Longitudinal Waves (e.g., sound waves).
Medium

4. Period: If a wave has a frequency of 50 Hz, what is its period?

Answer
T = 1 / f = 1 / 50 = 0.02 seconds.

🔴 Hard

Hard

5. Wave Interactions: What happens to the wavelength of a wave if the frequency is doubled, assuming the wave speed remains constant?

Answer
Since v = f * λ, if speed is constant and frequency doubles, the wavelength must be halved (1/2).
