🔊 Waves & Sound

Easy

1. Navigation: How do bats navigate and find prey in the dark?

View Answer
Echolocation. They emit high-frequency sound waves and listen for the echoes bouncing off objects.
Easy

2. Wave Source: What is the source of all wave motion?

View Answer
Vibration.
Medium

3. Doppler Effect: You are standing still and a siren moves towards you. What happens to the frequency you hear?

View Answer
The frequency increases (the pitch sounds higher).

💡 Light & Optics

Easy

4. EM Speed: What is the speed of all electromagnetic waves in a vacuum?

View Answer
3.0 x 10⁸ m/s (the speed of light).
Medium

5. Color Mixing: What are the three primary colors of light, and what color do they make when added together?

View Answer
Red, Blue, and Green. They are additive primaries and combine to make White light.
Hard

6. Refraction: A light ray travels from water (high density) into air (low density) at an angle. Does it bend toward or away from the normal?

View Answer
It bends away from the normal. (Fast to Slow = Toward; Slow to Fast = Away).

⚡ Electricity & Magnetism

Easy

7. Van de Graaff: Why does your hair stand up when you touch a Van de Graaff generator?

View Answer
Because like charges repel. Your hairs all gain the same charge and push away from each other.
Medium

8. Circuit Types: In a house, if one lightbulb burns out, the rest stay on. Are the lights wired in series or parallel?

View Answer
Parallel. Parallel circuits provide multiple paths for current, so one break doesn't stop the whole system.
Hard

9. Transformers: A transformer has 50 turns on the primary coil and 1,000 turns on the secondary coil. What type of transformer is it, and what does it do to the voltage?

View Answer
It is a Step-up Transformer. It increases the voltage (and decreases the current).

⚛️ Nuclear Physics

Easy

10. Half-Life: An isotope has a half-life of 3 days. After 9 days, how much of the original sample remains?

View Answer
1/8th.
3 days = 1/2
6 days = 1/4
9 days = 1/8.
Medium

11. Stability: Why are large nuclei (like Uranium) often unstable?

View Answer
The nucleus is too large for the Strong Nuclear Force to counteract the Electrostatic Repulsion (protons pushing each other away).
Hard

12. Fusion vs Fission: Where does nuclear fusion naturally occur, and what is the primary difference from fission?

View Answer
Fusion occurs in the Sun/Stars.
Fusion is the combining of small nuclei into a larger one.
Fission is the splitting of a large nucleus into smaller ones.
