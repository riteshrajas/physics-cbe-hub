🚀 Describing Motion: Practice Questions

Prepare for your Physics Credit By Exam with these 9 targeted questions.

🟢 Easy Questions

Easy

1. Speed Calculation: A student rides their bike 1,500 meters to school in 300 seconds. What is the student's average speed?

View Answer
Use v = d / t
v = 1500m / 300s = 5 m/s
Easy

2. Distance vs. Displacement: A track athlete runs exactly one full lap around a 400-meter circular track. What is their total distance traveled and their total displacement?

View Answer
Distance: 400m (Total path length)
Displacement: 0m (They returned to the starting point)
Easy

3. Acceleration: A car at a red light starts moving and reaches a velocity of 20 m/s in 4 seconds. What is the car's average acceleration?

View Answer
Use a = Δv / t
a = (20 m/s - 0 m/s) / 4s = 5 m/s²

🟡 Medium Questions

Medium

4. Velocity & Direction: An explorer walks 30 meters East, then turns and walks 40 meters North. If the entire trip takes 50 seconds, what is the magnitude of their average velocity?

View Answer
1. Find Displacement (hypotenuse): √(30² + 40²) = 50m.
2. Use v = Δx / t
v = 50m / 50s = 1 m/s
Medium

5. Deceleration: A truck traveling at 30 m/s slams on its brakes and comes to a complete stop in 6 seconds. What is the truck's acceleration?

View Answer
Use a = (v_final - v_initial) / t
a = (0 - 30) / 6 = -5 m/s² (The negative sign indicates deceleration)
Medium

6. Graph Interpretation: On a Position-vs-Time graph, the line is a straight diagonal starting at (0,0) and passing through (10s, 50m). What is the velocity, and what would the corresponding Velocity-vs-Time graph look like?

View Answer
Velocity = slope = 50m / 10s = 5 m/s.
The Velocity-vs-Time graph would be a horizontal line at 5 m/s (constant velocity).

🔴 Hard Questions

Hard

7. Frame of Reference: You are on a train moving North at 20 m/s. You walk toward the back of the train at 2 m/s relative to the train. What is your velocity relative to a person standing still on the train tracks outside?

View Answer
Combine the vectors: 20 m/s (North) + 2 m/s (South).
Net velocity = 18 m/s North.
Hard

8. Multi-stage Motion: A car travels at a constant velocity of 10 m/s for 5 seconds, then accelerates at 2 m/s² for the next 5 seconds. What is the car's final velocity at the end of the 10 seconds?

View Answer
The first 5 seconds don't change the velocity (constant 10 m/s).
Use v_f = v_i + at for the second stage:
v_f = 10 m/s + (2 m/s² * 5s) = 10 + 10 = 20 m/s.
Hard

9. Graph Analysis (Area): An object's motion is shown on a Velocity-vs-Time graph. The velocity increases uniformly from 0 m/s to 12 m/s over a period of 4 seconds. Calculate the total displacement of the object.

View Answer
Displacement = Area under the V-t graph. This forms a triangle.
Area = 1/2 * base * height
Displacement = 1/2 * 4s * 12 m/s = 24 meters.
